About Me

This is where I describe myself in a few paragraphs, and cajole you into supporting my work. Strap in.

What’s the point of this site?

I’m writing as therapy, as social outreach, and as livelihood in the spirit of mutual aid, honesty, recovery, growth. Please contact me if you read something you love (/hate)… or if you find any speeling mistaks. I welcome your feedback.


In one word, this project is about change. The universal quality of all phenomena. The cause of - and solution to - all our suffering and pain. Also needed to do laundry, and use the damn shopping cart at Superstore.


Change is the heart of this work in the sense that I have, and am and will continue to. I’d like to share in the process with you. Let’s change together (and get weird).


Knowing nod. Tip of the hat.

Disclosure

I’m an adult survivor of childhood emotional neglect and religious abuse, as well as sexual assault, addiction, and homelessness. I’ve been diagnosed C-PTSD and other stuff related to trauma and anxiety.


My disorder bingo card is pretty full. I’ve been in therapy for coming up on two years, but should’ve been for the past twenty. It’s been a colorful life, I’ve traveled a lot of hard miles and had some very rough experiences, but I try not to let all that define me.


My hope is that this project will help grow our mutual empathy and compassion. I do my best not to engage in pure catharsis or defeatist mental masturbation, nobody needs to see that. I work extremely hard at finding the positive, and sometimes I blow it - I know I’m not unique in that challenge. We’re in progress and that’s okay.


I identify as neurodiverse. My pronouns are he/him/hey you. My alter ego’s name is Blammo and he’s a psychedelic clown from outer space - an agent of good trouble, cellular sarcasm, and fine chaos. I write semi-anonymously under the name Gamma Cray so that people I don’t like can’t find my stuff and try to contact me.


I’m trained in Mental Health First-Aid, Psychedelic First-Aid, actual First-Aid, and Crisis Intervention. More to come. Please feel free to reach out to me if you’d like to connect. I take privacy and anonymity very seriously and if you contact me, I hope you do as well.
